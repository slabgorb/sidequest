### Devil's Advocate
Argue this code is broken. First attack: the deletions hide a live dependency. Could something import `pygame` lazily — an `importlib.import_module("pygame")`, an entry-point plugin, a `__import__` in a string, a test that shells out? I grepped only literal `pygame`; a dynamic loader would slip past. Mitigant: preflight ran the *full* 431-test suite green against the regenerated lockfile in a venv where pygame is now absent — a lazy import would surface as a runtime ImportError under test, and none did. The dep was a leaf with no transitive dependents removed that anything else needs (lock check passed). Second attack: `uv.lock` regeneration could have silently bumped *other* pinned versions, smuggling an unrelated upgrade into a "housekeeping" diff. The diff shows only pygame-ce + its own wheels removed (17 lines), and `uv lock --check` reports no drift — so no stealth bumps. Third attack: deleting `ml/__init__.py` orphans `__pycache__` or breaks namespace-package resolution elsewhere. Verified the dir is gone entirely and no `sidequest_daemon.ml` import exists anywhere. Fourth, and the one that landed: the *documentation* is the product here, and a confused future author — Jade adding homebrew, or Keith six months out — reads `CLAUDE.md:121`, trusts that `types.py` exports `StageCue/RenderTier/RenderResult`, and imports from the wrong module. The doc's whole job is to prevent exactly that misdirection, and this revision encodes a fresh one. A stressed reader skims the tree, not the source; the tree lies. That is the failure this story was opened to fix, reintroduced one line below the correct entry. Fifth attack: are the "out of scope" stale refs (`audio` at line 23, `memory_manager` in ADR prose) actually load-bearing? No — they mislead but don't break imports, and the daemon-repo file is genuinely outside this diff. They are correctly non-blocking. The devil's strongest point stands: a doc-accuracy story cannot ship with a new doc inaccuracy in its headline artifact.
