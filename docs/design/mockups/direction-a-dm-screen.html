Chapter III · The Lichgate

Act II · Scene 3 · dusk

You crossed the plaza at dusk. The lamplighter was gone. The gate had not yet been barred.

A woman waited beneath it, her hands crossed at her waist. She did not turn when you approached.

THE ASH-WOMAN

She hasn't blinked. You are close enough now to see the veins beneath her skin — not blue, but the pale white of old ivory. A small breeze lifts the grey of her hair and sets it down again in exactly the same place.

"You are late, Rux of the Bastion. I have been waiting three nights and a morning."

The gate behind her is seamed with frost that should not be there in summer. Somewhere, far back in the city, a bell begins to count the hour.
