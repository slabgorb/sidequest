Ironvale Mines Turn 7

The descent into the mines began at dawn, when the last traces of mist still clung to the valley floor. Brother Aldric blessed the threshold with salt and iron filings while the rest of the company checked their provisions one final time.

The first level proved unremarkable — abandoned carts, rusted picks, the remnants of a mining operation halted decades ago. But the air grew heavier with each flight of stairs, and by the third landing, even the torches seemed reluctant to burn.

[ scene image — the merchant's underground stall ]

The merchant sits cross-legged atop a threadbare rug, his wares arranged in careful rows upon a salvaged door laid flat across two barrels. Tallow candles gutter at each corner, throwing unsteady light across an assortment of oddments — tarnished pendants, small leather pouches that clink when disturbed, a hand-drawn map pinned open with iron nails.

He watches you approach with eyes that catch the candlelight like a cat's. "Careful, friend," he says, not looking up from the blade he's oiling. "Everything here has a price. And I don't mean gold."

A faint scratching comes from behind the stone wall to your left — too rhythmic to be rats, too quiet to be mining. The merchant doesn't react.
