[ FEED: sub-level 7 — elevator bay ]

The elevator dropped you into Sub-Level 7 with a hydraulic scream that scattered the rats. Down here, the neon doesn't reach — just the amber emergency strips bolted every ten meters along the ceiling, half of them dead.

The air tastes like ozone and old copper. Your neural HUD flickered twice on the descent — interference from the shielding, or something else scanning you.

[ FEED: corridor — emergency lighting ]

Your mesh sniffer lights up like a holiday display. Twelve distinct signals, most of them encrypted corporate dead-drops running automated handshakes with nobody. But three are live — two personal devices and something bigger, something drawing serious power from behind a reinforced door at the corridor's end.

Kira's voice crackled through your cochlear implant: "The data broker operates out of a converted water treatment plant. Third corridor, left at the junction marked with red graffiti. And Zyx? Don't let him scan your optics."

▸ I head for the red graffiti junction, keeping to the shadows
[ FEED: junction — surveillance eye ]

The junction is easy to find — someone's sprayed a three-meter tall eye in fluorescent red across the wall, its pupil a camera lens that tracks you as you pass. Art project or surveillance? Down here, probably both.

The left corridor slopes downward. The air here is warmer — server heat, probably. You can hear the hum of hardware ahead, and underneath it, something that might be music or might be signal interference rendered as sound.

▸ I spoof the camera with my retinal spoof program before turning left
[ FEED: data broker's den — sub-level 7 ]

The data broker's den is exactly as depressing as advertised. Someone welded a sheet-metal awning over a busted storefront and lined the interior with salvaged server racks, their fans humming a discordant chorus. Cables snake across the floor like sleeping eels.

The broker himself sits behind a desk made from a slab of bulletproof glass resting on two gutted ATMs. He's old-school — no visible chrome except for the fiber optic cable running from behind his left ear directly into the wall. His eyes never quite focus on the same spot.

"You're the one Kira sent." It's not a question. His fingers drum a pattern on the glass. "I have what you need. Question is whether you have what I need. Creds are boring. I want the access codes to NovaCorp's employee mesh network. Sub-Level 3." He smiles. "Tick tock."
