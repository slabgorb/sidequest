The Circuit — Scorchflats Turn 9

Three days on the salt flats and the radiator was already singing a death rattle. You'd rationed the coolant, nursed the throttle, kept to the shade of the canyon walls where there was shade to keep to. But the Circuit doesn't forgive careful. Careful is just slow dying.

The convoy signal had been intermittent since dawn — three blips, then static, then nothing. Either they'd changed frequencies or they'd been hit. Either way, you were alone with the engine noise and the horizon.

[ the fuel depot — dusk ]

The fuel depot is a carcass of rusted tanks and stripped pumps baking under a sky the color of a bruise. Someone's living here — there's smoke from behind the main building, and a makeshift antenna lashed to the water tower with baling wire. Two vehicles sit in the yard: a flatbed missing its rear axle, and a motorcycle that looks like it still runs.

A figure steps out of the main building's shadow. Woman, maybe forty, built like someone who's been lifting engine blocks without help. She's got a wrench in one hand and a sawed-off in the other, and she's not pointing either at you yet.

"Kill the engine," she says. "We talk. Then maybe you get fuel, maybe you get gone. Depends on what you're carrying and where you're pointed."
