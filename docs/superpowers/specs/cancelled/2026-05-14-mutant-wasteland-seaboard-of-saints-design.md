# The Seaboard of Saints — World Design

**Genre:** `mutant_wasteland`
**World slug:** `seaboard_of_saints`
**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Status:** approved direction, design phase

---

## 1. Summary

A second world for the `mutant_wasteland` genre — *not* a Florida-style tacky-gonzo theme park. **The Seaboard of Saints** is a literary-gonzo post-plague Northeast: the Amtrak corridor from Down East Maine through Boston, Providence, the Hudson Valley, drowned New York, Philadelphia, Baltimore, and Washington D.C., fifteen centuries after the Glory plague broke the species line.

The world's central conceit is **iconographic mutation**: when the Glory rolled through, the water-system carried fragments of pre-war relics that *imprinted* mutation patterns on whomever drank from a given spring. Each pattern became a **Saint**. **New Catholicism** — the syncretic faith that emerged from Italian, Irish, Latin, and French-Canadian Catholic immigrant traditions overlaid with literary-figure veneration and 1848-era American Spiritualism (the Fox Sisters / Whitman Circle lineage) — recognizes the canon. The **Magisterium of the Seaboard** is both the doctrinal framework (the cosmology shared across the corridor) and the **Plenary-Council**-form ecclesial body (governance pattern from the real Baltimore Plenary Councils of 1852, 1866, and 1884) that adjudicates inter-Saint disputes.

The Seaboard is not a wasteland. Cities work. Trade runs the corridor. Riverboats ply the Hudson, lighthouse-keepers tend the coast, the Catskills resorts open in summer and Sleepers thaw on schedule. PCs are adventurers in a *functioning weird world*, not survivors of a dying one.

The work also includes a **genre-level chargen overhaul** for `mutant_wasteland` — replacing the current 5-step menu with a 7-step intricate flow modeled on Gamma World 4e and Caves of Qud. The Saint-Bundle mutation system, defects-for-power asymmetry, and Sleeper/Mutant/Animal/Plant/Synthetic stock paths apply to all worlds in the genre (flickering_reach inherits).

## 2. Design context

### Why now
The user wants a second mutant_wasteland world more literary and intricate than flickering_reach, drawing on Caves of Qud, Gamma World 4e, Rimworld, *The Einstein Intersection*, *A Canticle for Leibowitz*, *12 Monkeys*, Spielberg's *A.I.*, and Woody Allen's *Sleeper*. The existing chargen is too thin to support the requested texture; rebuilding at genre level is the right scope.

### What we're not building
- A survival game. No carry-weight, no thirst, no rad-counter, no encumbrance. Travel and resupply are **smash cuts**, not played scenes.
- A monoculture. Each region has its own register; *most regions work*; PCs are adventurers within a functioning world, not survivors of a collapsing one.
- A tacky-gonzo world. Florida-style brand-density (Disney, NASCAR, animatronic mascots, Mar-a-Lago) is the failure mode. The strangeness must come from historical/cultural *depth*, not from marketing iconography.

### Tonal target
Gonzo Gamma World register **plus** the Spielberg-A.I. + Sleeper register of *comic dystopia where life works fine for non-protagonists*. Things still operate, just absurdly. The wahoo is Henny Youngman thawing at Brown's Hotel and asking which *tummler* is on tonight, not animatronic mice.

## 3. The Seaboard — geography

### High concept
The corridor from Penobscot Bay to the Potomac. Fifteen centuries after the **Glory** plague and the **Shake** software-cascade, the rugged Northeast infrastructure has held up better than most of the continent. Lighthouses still rotate. The MTA still runs (partially). The Library of Congress still catalogues. The lower coast is partially submerged from climate-shift; the upper coast is largely intact.

### Regions

The Seaboard is structured around **the Corridor** — the still-mostly-functional pre-war rail line that connects most settled cities. Movement along the Corridor is fast (the trains still run, on a schedule nobody understands); movement off the Corridor is the adventure.

## 4. Cosmology — New Catholicism and the Magisterium

### The Glory as theological event
The Glory plague is canonized as **the Annunciation**: the moment new patron saints were revealed by being bound into the water system. Different traditions read this differently:

- **The Magisterium of the Seaboard** (the syncretic Catholic mainstream): the Glory was God revising the canon. Italian, Irish, Latin, and French-Canadian Catholic immigrant traditions had been venerating folk-saints alongside the official Vatican canon for centuries; the Glory simply *made it official*. The Magisterium meets in **Plenary Council** form — the governance pattern is borrowed from the real Baltimore Plenary Councils of 1852, 1866, and 1884, which are canonical American-Catholic history. Principal sees: Providence, NYC, Boston, Philadelphia, and Brookland (D.C.). The Magisterium adjudicates Saint recognition, inter-Saint marriage rules, miracle disputes, and the canonical Saint Registry.
- **The Sisters of the Whitman Circle at the Athenaeum** (Providence-based, Spiritualist-Bibliographic): the Glory was a *manuscript revision*. The Sisters preserve pre-war scripture (newspapers, sheet music, technical manuals, novels) as canonical text alongside the bible; conduct authenticated seances in the 1848-Fox-Sisters lineage; authorize Magisterium-approved automatic-writing. Library-as-faith.
- **The Brahmin Protestant remnant** (Boston Common, the Adirondack hermits, the Plymouth elders): the Glory was a test that the Catholics passed and the Protestants failed; they are humble about it, but the strain is there. The **1834 Ursuline Convent burning at Charlestown** (an actual Protestant mob burned an actual Ursuline convent — perfect post-apoc resonance) is the unhealed historical wound that the strain is built on. The Saturday Club observes the anniversary every August 11 with what they describe as "regret expressed in due form."
- **The Lo'in** (next section): the Glory *unmade* humanity into archetypes; saints aren't divine, they're *roles*.
- **The Sleepers' Sodality**: confused. Many converted on waking. Saint Henny has a chapel at Brown's Hotel.

### Doctrinal mechanism for Wild Mutants
The Magisterium recognizes Wild Mutants as **un-marked, not heretical**. The administrative consequence: Wild Mutants may receive baptism and burial rites but **not** the Saint-rites of marriage — that requires a Saint-mark on both parties. Provisional marking is available after a **year's catechumenate** at a parish; the local bishop adjudicates whether the candidate's pattern matches an existing Saint or merits a new Wild-derived Saint petition (rare — only one new Saint has been added to the canon by this route in the last 200 years: Saint Florica of the Bronx, 1851 Romanian-Romani extraction, recognized 1979). The Athenaeum Sisters consider Wild Mutants *unsigned manuscripts* awaiting attribution; the Saturday Club considers them *honest*; the Lo'in regard them as *cast members without scripts*.

### What this gives us mechanically
- A consistent in-world vocabulary for mutation (*Saint-marked, mark-bearer, the Saint's drawback, walking under the patron's hand*)
- A framework for faction politics (the Magisterium / Athenaeum / Brahmin / Lo'in disagree about doctrine, not just resources)
- A natural way to introduce real-Earth historical figures as in-world Saints without breaking the fiction
- A reason for the genre's confrontation list to include **social** types prominently (parish politics, doctrinal dispute, miracle adjudication)

## 5. Stocks — the multi-path chargen entry

**Six stocks**, each a different mechanical asymmetry. *Replaces* the current implicit Mutant Human / Pure Strain Human / Plant Person / Uplifted Animal / Synthetic / Hybrid list with a clearer set:

1. **Saint-Marked** — Born to a village whose water carries a Saint's relic. Gets that Saint's mutation bundle (3-4 mutations) and that Saint's drawback. Can take additional drawbacks for additional mutations from the same Saint's affinity list. *Most common stock.*
2. **Wild Mutant** — Born off the water-system or in a region with mixed-up springs. Rolls random mutation pulls (or picks from a buffet) with full defects-for-power flexibility. No Saint, no social legibility, more mechanical freedom. *Outsider register.*
3. **Sleeper** — Pre-war human, cryogenically preserved, thawed. The Pure Strain Human stock. No mutations; *implants instead* — pre-war medical augments still in working order (subdermal weave, cortex booster, optic suite, etc.). Bewildered. Comic-dystopia register. *Pure Strain reskinned with depth.*
4. **Animal-Stock** — Uplifted Northeast fauna. Penobscot- and Wampanoag-language names where the stock is anchored in Indigenous-territory regions (Down East and Plymouth/Cape Cod/Mashpee): **muwin-folk** (Penobscot for bear; Adirondacks and Maine Woods), **kiwhosis-folk** (Penobscot for muskrat; Hudson Valley wetlands), **ahtuhq-folk** (Wampanoag for deer; Plymouth Stone region), **paquanaog-folk** (Wampanoag for clam-people; Cape Cod). Plus English-named stocks where the Indigenous registers don't reach: lobster-folk (Down East coastal), harbor-seal-folk (Whalecoast), cardinal-folk (Hudson Valley), mountain-lion-folk (Catskills), fox-folk (Pioneer Valley, Catskills), brook-trout-folk (Adirondack streams). Retains animal traits. Picks a base creature plus one Saint affinity. *The Penobscot-language naming acknowledges that uplift is recognized by the actually-extant Indigenous polities — not a flattening detail.*
5. **Plant-Stock** — White-pine-folk, sugar-maple-folk, salt-marsh-cord-grass-folk, mountain-laurel-folk, oak-folk. The Whalecoast has kelp-folk and the Hudson has cattail-folk. Largely Adirondack-resident; visiting cities is an event.
6. **Synthetic** — Pre-war animatronic figures who achieved (or had imposed on them) personhood. MTA conductor automatons, Smithsonian docent units, Catskills band-stand brass-bots, NYPL reference librarian models. Subsystem-based progression (heat-sink, signal-rider, etc.).

Each stock has different starting mutation/implant rules, different social legibility, and different starting affiliations. The chargen flow branches at step 2 by stock.

### Wild (Misborn)
No Saint. Random rolls. Maximum mechanical freedom; minimum social legibility. The Magisterium considers Wild Mutants **un-marked**, not heretical; the Athenaeum considers them *unsigned manuscripts*; the Brahmin Protestant remnant considers them *honest*. The Lo'in regard them as *cast members without scripts.*

### Region binding (loosened per user feedback)
Saints have *patron regions* — places where their water-relic concentrates and where their veneration is centered. **But mutants migrate.** A Saint Marie of Salem can move to the South Bronx. A Saint Roberto-marked mutant can take orders at the Athenaeum. Cross-Saint marriages are common; their children get the **dominant** Saint by water-exposure (where the mother bathed during pregnancy), not by inheritance. The regional flavor exists but is *not deterministic*.

This matters because:
- It prevents the "everyone in Boston is a Brahmin-Saint" failure mode the user flagged.
- It gives narrative texture (the Saint-Marie immigrant in Spanish Harlem trying to find a witch-coven).
- It supports faction politics (the Magisterium adjudicates inter-Saint disputes weekly).

## 7. Factions

Six primary factions; each can sponsor PCs, oppose PCs, or be ignored.

1. **The Magisterium of the Seaboard** — **Plenary-Council**-form Catholic syncretic church (the form is borrowed from the actual Baltimore Plenary Councils of 1852, 1866, and 1884, the canonical American-Catholic governance pattern). Principal sees in Providence (Athenaeum), NYC (St. Patrick's-equivalent), Boston (Holy Cross-equivalent), Philadelphia, and D.C. (Brookland — "Little Rome"). Administers Saint recognition, doctrinal disputes, marriage between Saint-marks, miracle adjudication, and the canonical Saint Registry. Open conflict with the Brahmin Protestant remnant over the unhealed 1834 Ursuline Convent burning at Charlestown — an actual Protestant mob burned an actual Catholic convent and the Magisterium has never forgotten.
2. **The Sisters of the Whitman Circle at the Athenaeum** — *replaces "Sisters of the Memorabilia."* Providence-based monastic order, named for **Sarah Helen Whitman** (1803-1878, the real Providence Spiritualist poet whom Poe courted in 1848 at the actual Providence Athenaeum reading room). Their cosmology is library-as-faith + 1848-era American Spiritualism (Fox Sisters lineage) + the Whitman Circle of Providence intellectuals + bibliographic monasticism. They preserve pre-war scripture (newspapers, sheet music, technical manuals, novels), conduct authenticated seances, and authorize Magisterium-approved automatic-writing. Junior houses at the Library of Congress (D.C.), the New York Public Library, the Boston Athenaeum, the Mount Holyoke library. *(The Canticle-influenced faction, but renamed away from Miller's coinage to a Providence-specific anchor.)*
3. **The Lo'in** — Mutant performers who reenact pre-war literary, musical, and cinematic myth in mutated form. Lo Melville-at-the-Whale, Lo Hester-of-the-Letter, Lo Edgar-of-the-Raven, Lo Sinatra-of-the-Stage. *(Einstein Intersection. Pilgrimage to Lovecraft's grave at Swan Point is theirs — he never wrote about them as monsters, so they reclaim him.)*
4. **The Sleepers' Sodality** — *replaces "Sleepers' Council."* Catholic-immigrant-style mutual-aid sodality (the *sodality* form is the real Catholic-American confraternity register — Sodality of the Blessed Virgin Mary, etc., 19th-century parish mutual-aid). Thawed pre-war humans organize for mutual-aid, legal advocacy (Sleeper rights are still being settled in some parishes), pre-war coupon redemption services. Headquartered at the Brown's Hotel in the Catskills with a junior house at Maryhouse on the Lower East Side.
5. **The Saturday Club** — *replaces "Brahmin Privy Council."* Boston Common's old aristocracy administered through Emerson's real 1855 dinner club (Saturday Club: Emerson, Longfellow, Hawthorne, Holmes Sr., Lowell, Agassiz). The Somerset Club (1851, 42 Beacon Street, real) and "The Vault" (the real 1959-99 informal Boston business oligarchy, recently revived) are subsidiary bodies. Long-blooded Saint-Brahmin marks dominate; the order is insular, courteous, devastating. Owns much of the Corridor real estate.
6. **The Patroon Houses** — Hudson Valley feudal-pastoral. Real Dutch West India Company 1629 manorial grants: **Van Rensselaer of Rensselaerswijck** (the senior house, Albany), **Livingston of Clermont** (mid-Hudson), **Philipse of Philipsburg Manor** (Tarrytown), **Beekman**, **Van Cortlandt**, **Schuyler**. The 1839-1845 **Anti-Rent War** (real — tenant farmers in disguise as "Calico Indians" fought the Patroon system) is canonical in-world conflict precedent. Each manor-state hosts a Saint of their preference. *Crowley's Little, Big faerie register sits on top.*

**Minor factions** (region-specific):
- **The Sternmen Guilds** of Down East (pound-keepers and lobster co-ops)
- **The Mount Washington Observatory** (weather-priests)
- **The Cape Ann Sodality** (Folly Cove / Lanesville / Pigeon Cove fishing-villages confraternity)
- **The Smithsonian Reliquary Order** (Memorabilia junior in D.C., Brookland-affiliated)
- **The Atwells Avenue Society** — *replaces "Mafia Council of Federal Hill"* — modeled on the real Patriarca crime family that headquartered at Federal Hill from the 1950s-90s; syncretized with the Magisterium in their own arrangement
- **The Mashpee-Aquinnah Council** — *replaces "Wampanoag Confederacy"* — the two actual federally-recognized Wampanoag tribal nations (Mashpee on the Cape, Aquinnah on Martha's Vineyard's western cliffs), in real-world standing as sovereign polities
- **The Red Hook Stevedores** — *replaces "South Brooklyn Dispatch"* — longshoremen-and-smugglers; Budd Schulberg's *On the Waterfront* register; the Red Hook Houses survived the Sunken Bight by elevation
- **The Catskills Resorts Guild** (Brown's / Grossinger's / Concord / Kutsher's / Nevele / Pines association)
- **The Tubman Trails Society** (Underground-Railroad route-keepers, Maryland Eastern Shore through Philadelphia)
- **The Cabrini Missionary Sisters** (Mother Cabrini's actual order, still founding institutions across the Corridor)

## 8. Currency — Coins

Pre-war US coins. The Glory was a plague, not a bomb — pennies, nickels, dimes, quarters, half-dollars, and Sacagawea dollars survived the collapse with their metal intact. Coins are durable, denominated, ubiquitous, and **the United States Mint at Philadelphia (real, founded 1792, oldest in the country) still strikes them** by Magisterium decree. The Mint's automated minting line was hardened against the Shake software-cascade and never went dark. New strikes follow pre-Cataclysm dies because nobody has dared to change them.

The theological joke writes itself: *In God We Trust* on the quarter is read as a Magisterium endorsement of the Federal Coinage; the eagle on the reverse is Saint Eagle of the Reverse (a folk-Saint, recognized informally); Sacagawea is canonized.

Denominations matter — a roll of quarters carries a full transaction; pennies are alms. The Saint-Marked tithe in pennies to their parish; the Brahmin Privy negotiates in half-dollar rolls; the Patroons honor old silver dollars and refuse new strikes.

**Secondary currencies** (regional):
- **Whaling Shares** (Whalecoast / Down East) — fractional "lays" of a catch, real 1840s Melville-era New Bedford whaling system
- **Lighthouse Letters** (Down East) — drafts redeemable at automated lighthouse stations
- **Athenaeum Vellum** (Providence and learned circles) — manuscript-backed scrip
- **Mass Pike Tokens** (interstate-corridor segments) — *real Mass Pike toll tokens were used until 1998*; surviving stock circulates along the corridor
- **Sleepers' Coupons** (pre-war retail coupons still honored by thawed Sleeper businesses) — comic-mechanical

**Explicitly not present**: no bottlecaps, no MTA-tokens-as-cutout-for-bottlecaps, no water-as-currency, no nuka-cola-equivalent, no rare-metal hoarding cult.

## 9. Genre-level chargen overhaul (7-step)

Replaces the existing 5-step in `mutant_wasteland/char_creation.yaml`. **All worlds in the genre inherit** — flickering_reach gets the new chargen for free.

### The seven steps

1. **Stock** *(Saint-Marked / Wild Mutant / Sleeper / Animal-Stock / Plant-Stock / Synthetic)*. Determines branching of step 2.
2. **Mutations / Implants / Subsystems** — the chargen pivot:
   - Saint-Marked: pick a Saint; receive its bundle and drawback; optionally take additional drawbacks from the Saint's affinity list for additional mutations
   - Wild Mutant: roll **2 physical + 1 mental** from world-augmented tables (Seaboard's tables include both shared genre items and Saint-affinity items); optionally take **mental defects** (Phobia, Drain, Visible Stigma, Drift) for additional mutations
   - Sleeper: pick a **starting implant** (subdermal weave / cortex booster / optic suite / dermal vox / blood-filter) with one open slot for future install
   - Animal-Stock: pick a base creature; receive its natural-ability set plus one Saint affinity
   - Plant-Stock: pick a base plant; receive sessile-mobility plus one Saint affinity
   - Synthetic: pick a **subsystem** (heat-sink / signal-rider / weight-shifter / archive-mind / drone-host)
3. **Calling** *(class)* — existing genre list, restricted by stock: **Scavenger, Mutant, Pureblood, Wirewalker, Beastkin, Tinker** (as currently defined in `mutant_wasteland/rules.yaml`). One *new* Calling added at genre level: **Penitent** — Magisterium-aligned PC, gets miracle-narration social-confrontation beats but takes a vow-drawback. The existing Calling list is retained verbatim; Penitent is additive.
4. **Attributes** — 27-point-buy default (existing rule). Add **"Roll the Bones"** alternate mode (3d6+3 per stat, place in order; reroll budget of two stats; embraces the GW4 "hopeless character" possibility).
5. **Cryptic Alliance** — pick a faction tie or *Unaligned*. World-specific list. Affects opening NPC reactions and a starting equipment slot.
6. **Origin** — world-specific narrative tag: which city, parish, manor, or village. Equivalent of flickering_reach's "Where You Woke Up" but extended.
7. **Artifact** — the existing pick, expanded by world. Some artifacts gated by Stock (Sleepers come with pre-war ID cards; Synthetics come with a charger).

### Drawback-for-Power asymmetry
The Qud-derived mechanic. Saint-Marked and Wild Mutants can purchase additional mutations by accepting physical or mental defects. Defects have **mechanical consequences in confrontations** (Phobia gives the opponent a free angle; Stigma reduces social-confrontation starting metric; Drain costs Edge per mutation use).

### What the new chargen does NOT do
- Force pre-set "five origins" menus — chargen is now branching, not linear-multiple-choice
- Require random rolls; rolls are an *option* alongside point-buy and pick
- Add new attributes; the existing six (Brawn / Reflexes / Toughness / Wits / Instinct / Presence) are retained
- Replace the existing confrontation list
